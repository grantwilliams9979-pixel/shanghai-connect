Payments Guide

Mobile payments in China for foreigners.

Cash is almost gone in Shanghai. Alipay and WeChat Pay handle nearly everything — and both work with overseas bank cards. Here is how to get set up.

Why this matters

Shanghai runs on QR codes

Taxis, restaurants, convenience stores, markets, metro, and most small shops expect mobile payment. Many vendors no longer accept cash or foreign credit cards at the point of sale. Setting up at least one mobile payment method in your first 48 hours removes most day-to-day payment friction.

Two apps to know

Most foreigners start with Alipay because the foreign card setup is slightly more straightforward. WeChat Pay is equally important once you are using WeChat for day-to-day communication.

The basics

How mobile payments work in China

  • Link Link an overseas bank card

    Both Alipay and WeChat Pay accept Visa, Mastercard, and some other international cards. There are spending limits for foreign cards — typically around ¥3,000 to ¥50,000 per month depending on verification level.
  • Verify Complete identity verification

    Basic features work without full verification, but linking a card and raising spending limits requires passport verification. This can take a few minutes or up to 24 hours.
  • Pay Scan or show your QR code

    Either scan the merchant's QR code yourself, or display your personal payment QR code for them to scan. Both methods work in most shops, restaurants, and transport.

Practical tips

What to know before your first payment

  • 01 Set up Alipay before you need it

    Verification sometimes takes time. Do not wait until you are standing at a till. Set up and test a small transaction on your first day.
  • 02 Use a stable Chinese phone number

    Payment verification codes go to your registered phone number. Using a local Chinese number or a stable eSIM prevents delays and lockouts.
  • 03 Turn your VPN off before paying

    Both Alipay and WeChat Pay can flag or block payments made while a VPN is active. Disable your VPN for payment transactions, then re-enable it afterwards.
  • 04 Keep a backup card for emergencies

    Some hotels, large international retailers, and airport shops accept foreign credit cards directly. Keep one physical card available for situations where mobile payment fails.
