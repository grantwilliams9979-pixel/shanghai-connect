Arrival Guide

Shanghai arrival checklist.

The essential steps to get settled in Shanghai — in the right order. Each item links to a detailed guide.

Before arrival On arrival First week

Do before you fly

Before arrival

These steps are significantly harder to complete after you land. Do them at home.

  • 01 Install your VPN

    Choose a provider, install the app, and test it before you travel. VPN apps are blocked in China and cannot be downloaded after you land.
  • 02 Download essential apps

    Install WeChat, Alipay, Didi, Baidu Maps, and Google Translate before you fly. Download offline translation packs for maps and menus.
  • 03 Plan your SIM or eSIM

    Decide between a local China SIM card and an international eSIM so you are connected the moment you land — not searching for airport Wi-Fi.

First 48 hours

On arrival

Get connected, set up payments, and get to your accommodation without friction.

  • 04 Activate your SIM or eSIM

    Get a local phone number. Most WeChat and Alipay features require a stable Chinese mobile number for verification codes.
  • 05 Set up Alipay

    Link your overseas bank card, complete identity verification, and confirm you can pay in shops before your first errand. Cash is increasingly rare in Shanghai.
  • 06 Learn the metro

    The Shanghai metro covers most of the city and connects both airports. Learn how to pay, which apps to use, and how to plan routes.
  • 07 Book your first Didi

    Set up Didi and test a short trip. Knowing how to book a ride and how to show your destination in Chinese saves a lot of stress early on.

First week

First week

Housing, groceries, healthcare, and the daily routines that make Shanghai feel manageable.

  • 08 Choose a neighbourhood

    If you have not signed a lease yet, compare Jing'an, Xuhui, Pudong, Changning, Huangpu, and Minhang before committing. Commute and school access should come first.
  • 09 Find your nearest international supermarket

    Locate the closest City Super, Ole, Hema, or international supermarket to your home. Set up at least one grocery delivery app for convenience.
  • 10 Know your healthcare options

    Save the nearest international hospital and clinic in your phone before you need them. Confirm your insurance coverage and understand how to access expat-friendly healthcare.
