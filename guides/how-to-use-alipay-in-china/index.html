How to Use Alipay in China: Complete Guide for Foreigners

Download, set up, link your international card, pay by QR code, and use Alipay for transport in Shanghai — step by step.

Setup steps How to pay Limits & fees FAQ

Best for

Tourists, expats, international students, business travellers, and newcomers to Shanghai

Alipay is one of the first apps you should set up before arriving in China. In Shanghai, it is used for everyday payments, metro and bus rides, taxis, restaurants, convenience stores, shopping malls, hotels, tourist attractions, and many other daily services.

For foreigners, Alipay is especially important because many places in China rely heavily on QR code payments. International credit cards are not always accepted directly at smaller shops, restaurants, markets, or local service providers. Alipay helps bridge that gap by allowing many overseas visitors to link an international bank card and pay through the app.

Why it matters

Why Alipay is important in China

Alipay is more than a payment app. It is a daily-life app that helps you do many of the things you need as soon as you arrive in China.

You can use Alipay to:

  • Pay at shops, cafes, restaurants, supermarkets, and convenience stores
  • Scan QR codes at small local businesses
  • Use public transport in supported cities
  • Hail taxis or access ride-hailing services
  • Book hotels, flights, and train tickets
  • Use mini-programs without downloading separate apps
  • Check bills and payment history
  • Access translation and currency conversion tools in the international version

For Shanghai specifically, the official Shanghai government website describes Alipay as an essential app and a popular method for paying for things. It also lists WeChat Pay and Alipay as the two major digital payment methods in China. (english.shanghai.gov.cn)

Step-by-step setup

How to set up Alipay as a foreigner

Follow these four steps to get Alipay working with your international bank card.

  1. 01 Download and register

    Open the Alipay app and register using your mobile phone number. You do not always need a Chinese mainland phone number to get started. Shanghai's official guide notes that Alipay has streamlined registration for overseas visitors, removing the need to open a prepaid account or obtain a Chinese phone number. (english.shanghai.gov.cn)

  2. 02 Choose International Version

    After signing up, choose International Version if the app prompts you. This version is tailored for overseas travellers and includes services such as translation and currency conversion.

    Typical path: Me → Settings → Region → International Version. The exact wording may change depending on your app version.

  3. 03 Add your international bank card

    To add a card, open: Me → Bank Cards → Add Bank Card, then follow the in-app instructions.

    Visa, Mastercard, Diners Club International, and Discover are supported networks. (english.shanghai.gov.cn)

  4. 04 Complete identity verification

    Some users may be asked to complete identity verification before using all payment features. This usually involves entering your passport information and following the app's verification steps.

    Use your legal name exactly as it appears on your passport. If your bank card name and Alipay identity name do not match, card linking or payments may fail. Shanghai's payment guide specifically notes that the name on your credit card should match the registered name on your Alipay account for successful linkage and transactions. (english.shanghai.gov.cn)

Making payments

How to pay with Alipay

There are two main ways to pay with Alipay in China.

01

Merchant scans your code

Common at supermarkets, convenience stores, coffee shops, malls, and larger restaurants.

  1. Open Alipay
  2. Tap Pay / Receive
  3. Show your payment QR code to the cashier
  4. The cashier scans your code
  5. Confirm the payment if prompted
02

You scan the merchant's code

Common at smaller restaurants, street vendors, local shops, markets, and some taxis.

  1. Open Alipay
  2. Tap Scan
  3. Scan the merchant's QR code
  4. Enter the amount if required
  5. Confirm the payment

Getting around

Using Alipay for transport in Shanghai

Alipay can be used for public transport in supported cities, including transport-related services in Shanghai. Inside the app, look for Transport or 出行.

  1. 01 Open Alipay

    Make sure you have internet access before entering the metro station.

  2. 02 Tap Transport / 出行

    Select Shanghai if prompted to choose a city.

  3. 03 Activate the QR code

    Tap to generate the metro or bus QR code. This may take a moment on first use.

  4. 04 Scan at the gate

    Hold your phone screen up to the QR code scanner at the metro gate or bus scanner.

Recommended backup: Keep a small amount of RMB cash or a transport card available when you first arrive, especially before your Alipay account is fully tested.

Foreign card limitations

What foreigners can and cannot do with Alipay

Alipay is very useful for daily payments, but foreign-linked cards do not unlock every feature available to local Chinese users.

Feature Usually available to foreign card users? Notes
Pay merchants by QR code Yes Main use case
Pay in restaurants and shops Yes Works widely, but some failures can happen
Use transport QR codes Often yes Availability depends on city and setup
Link international bank card Yes Supported for many major card networks
Person-to-person transfers Usually no Not supported for international bank cards
Red packets Usually no Restricted for international bank cards
Wealth management / insurance No Not available for international card users
Full local banking-style functions No Requires local eligibility

International bank cards can be used for daily purchases in mainland China, but do not support person-to-person transfers, red packets, wealth management services, insurance services, or other financial services. (Alipay+)

Good to know

Fees, limits, and exchange rates

Payment rules can change, and fees may vary depending on the card, app version, payment method, and issuing bank. Always check the payment confirmation screen before paying.

China's official government website says the single transaction limit for overseas visitors using mobile payments was raised from USD 1,000 to USD 5,000, and the annual cumulative transaction cap was raised from USD 10,000 to USD 50,000. (State Council of China)

For exchange rates, payments using Alipay+ foreign exchange rates are updated and disclosed every business day, while exchange rates for international bank cards on Alipay are provided by the card scheme and issuing bank. (Alipay+)

Practical tip: Before making a large purchase, check whether your card issuer adds a foreign transaction fee. Your bank may charge separately from anything shown inside Alipay.

Which one?

Alipay vs WeChat Pay: which one should you use?

For newcomers to Shanghai, the best answer is: set up both Alipay and WeChat Pay.

Alipay is often easier for payments, transport, travel services, and some tourist-friendly features. WeChat Pay is important because WeChat is widely used for messaging, groups, restaurants, mini-programs, and local services.

Best setup for Shanghai newcomers:

  1. 01 Alipay

    For payments, transport, taxis, and travel services.

  2. 02 WeChat Pay

    For social payments, restaurant mini-programs, groups, and local services.

  3. 03 Some RMB cash

    As a backup, especially in your first few days.

  4. 04 One international card

    A card that works overseas, in case a digital payment fails.

  5. 05 Optional: local bank card

    Worth setting up if you are staying long term.

Pre-arrival checklist

Set up Alipay before arriving in China

Before your flight to Shanghai, try to complete this checklist.

  1. 01 Download official Alipay app

    From the App Store or Google Play. Check the developer name before downloading.

  2. 02 Register with your mobile phone number

    Your own number works — you do not need a Chinese number to register.

  3. 03 Switch to International Version

    Me → Settings → Region → International Version.

  4. 04 Add international bank card

    Me → Bank Cards → Add Bank Card. Use a Visa or Mastercard for best compatibility.

  5. 05 Complete passport verification if required

    Use your legal name exactly as it appears on your passport.

  6. 06 Check that Pay / Receive QR code opens

    Tap to make sure your payment code generates correctly.

  7. 07 Activate Shanghai transport QR code

    Tap Transport → Shanghai to activate it before you need it at the metro gate.

  8. 08 Test a small payment after arrival

    Try a small purchase at a convenience store to confirm your card works before a larger transaction.

Security

Safety tips for using Alipay

Use Alipay carefully, especially during your first few days in China.

  • Only download the official Alipay app.
  • Do not share your payment code screenshots.
  • Check the payment amount before confirming.
  • Keep your passport name and card name consistent.
  • Enable phone lock, Face ID, Touch ID, or passcode protection.
  • Keep a backup payment method.
  • Monitor your card statements for failed or duplicate charges.
  • Contact Alipay support or your bank if something looks wrong.

Alipay+ lists customer service numbers for payment-related questions, including a local China number and an overseas number, with service hours Monday to Sunday, 08:00–24:00 GMT+8. (Alipay+)

Frequently asked questions

Alipay questions for foreigners

Can foreigners use Alipay in China?

Yes. Foreign visitors can download Alipay, register, and link eligible international bank cards. Alipay+ says overseas users can choose International Version and bind a bank card to start using mobile payments across China. (Alipay+)

Do I need a Chinese bank account to use Alipay?

Not necessarily. Shanghai's official guide says Alipay has streamlined registration and verification for overseas visitors, eliminating the need to open a prepaid account or obtain a Chinese phone number. (english.shanghai.gov.cn)

Can I use Alipay with a foreign credit card?

Yes, many foreign visitors can link eligible international cards. Shanghai's official guide lists Visa, Mastercard, Diners Club International, and Discover as supported networks. (english.shanghai.gov.cn)

Can I use Alipay outside China?

Alipay's App Store and Google Play listings state that Alipay is not designed or intended for use by foreign users in their home country or outside China.

Can I send money to friends with Alipay?

Usually not with an international bank card. Alipay+ says international bank cards do not support person-to-person transfers or red packets. (Alipay+)

Is Alipay better than WeChat Pay?

Neither is strictly better. Alipay is excellent for payments, transport, travel, and tourist-friendly services. WeChat Pay is important because WeChat is deeply connected to communication, restaurants, groups, and local mini-programs. Most Shanghai newcomers should set up both.

Should I still carry cash in Shanghai?

Yes, at least at the beginning. Mobile payments are widely used, but cash is useful if your phone dies, your card is declined, your app verification fails, or a specific payment flow does not work.
