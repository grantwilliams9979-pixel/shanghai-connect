Getting around Shanghai.

Shanghai has one of the world's most extensive metro systems, reliable ride-hailing, affordable taxis, and bike sharing across the city. Here is how newcomers use it all.

The basics

Most journeys need just two things

For most daily life in Shanghai, the metro and Didi cover nearly everything. The metro is fast, cheap, and connects most of the city. Didi fills the gaps — late nights, heavy luggage, destinations not near a station, and journeys between lines. Learn these two first, then add the others as your routine settles.

Your main option

By metro

The Shanghai metro has over 20 lines and connects most areas expats live and work in. It accepts Alipay, WeChat Pay, metro card, and now foreign bank cards at many stations.

By taxi and Didi
