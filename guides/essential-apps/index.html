Essential Apps Starter Guide

The Shanghai apps to set up first.

A focused sequence for the first 48 hours, first week, and first month, plus the essential apps worth downloading before you fly.

Timeline Top apps Newcomer tips

MVP focus

Curated setup, not a giant directory

Start with the apps that unblock payment, messaging, transport, translation, and daily logistics. Everything else can wait until you have a local rhythm.

Setup timeline

What to install when

01

First 48 hours

Unlock payments, messaging, rides, maps, and translation before the first real errand.

  • WeChat: messaging, contacts, mini programs, and payments.
  • Alipay: foreign-card payments, transport QR, and city services.
  • Didi: airport rides and address-based taxi booking.
  • Baidu Maps: Chinese addresses, walking routes, and transit planning.
  • Google Translate: offline language backup before landing.
  • VPN app: choose, install, and test your provider before you travel.
02

First week

Add the apps that make food, metro, and real-life Chinese easier.

  • MetroMan: quick Shanghai metro routing and first/last train checks.
  • Meituan: restaurants, local services, and delivery discovery.
  • Pleco: reliable Chinese dictionary for signs, menus, and forms.
03

First month

Set up the bigger marketplaces and travel tools once your basics are stable.

  • Taobao: household goods, delivery habits, and local shopping.
  • Trip.com: domestic hotels, flights, trains, and English-language bookings.

Newcomer tips

A few setup rules save hours

Install early, verify slowly, and keep screenshots of Chinese addresses for your first week.

  1. 01 Download before arrival

    Install core apps and offline translation packs before you depend on airport Wi-Fi or roaming.

  2. 02 Use the same phone number

    Payment, delivery, ride-hailing, and hotel accounts are easier when verification codes reach one stable number.

  3. 03 Save addresses in Chinese

    Keep your hotel, apartment, school, or office address as Chinese text and as a screenshot.
