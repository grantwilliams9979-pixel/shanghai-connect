Living in Jing'an, Shanghai.

Central, well-connected, and full of international amenities. Jing'an is where most expats start — and many choose to stay.

Overview Metro and transport Housing Trade-offs

Best for

First-time arrivals who want convenience above everything

Jing'an sits in the heart of Puxi — west of the Bund, north of the Former French Concession, and directly connected to People's Square by Line 2. It has more international supermarkets, English-friendly restaurants, and easy transport links than almost any other part of Shanghai. If you are arriving for the first time and do not yet know which part of the city suits you, Jing'an is the lowest-friction starting point.

At a glance

What Jing'an is like

Location

Central Puxi

Jing'an district covers the area roughly between Nanjing West Road in the south, Suzhou Creek in the north, and Zhongshan Park in the west. The southern part — around Jing'an Temple and Nanjing West Road — is where most expats live.

  • Nanjing West Road — main shopping and dining strip
  • Jing'an Temple — central landmark and metro hub
  • Jiaozhou Road and Wuding Road — quieter residential streets
  • Kangding Road — popular with young professionals
Vibe

Polished and fast-paced

Jing'an feels like a proper international city neighbourhood. Busy streets, fast delivery, international brands alongside local options, and a strong weekend brunch scene. Less character than the FFC, more convenience than anywhere else.

  • International restaurants and cafes on most main streets
  • City Super and other international supermarkets nearby
  • Strong food delivery coverage (Meituan, Ele.me)
  • Busy during the week, lively on weekends
Best for

Who chooses Jing'an

Jing'an attracts expats who prioritise ease of setup, central access, and a walkable neighbourhood with strong expat infrastructure.

  • First-time arrivals to Shanghai or China
  • Young professionals and couples without children
  • People commuting to offices in Jing'an or Puxi
  • Those who want to walk to restaurants, shops, and transport

Getting around

Metro lines and transport

Jing'an has some of the best metro access in Shanghai. Multiple lines pass through the district, giving direct connections to most of the city.

  • Line 2 East–west across the city

    Jing'an Temple station connects directly to People's Square, Nanjing Road, Lujiazui in Pudong, and Pudong Airport. The most useful line for most daily journeys.
  • Line 7 North–south through Jing'an

    Passes through Changshu Road, Jing'an Temple, and Changping Road. Useful for reaching Xujiahui in the south and Changzhong Road in the north.
  • Lines 12 & 13 East–west through the district

    Lines 12 and 13 add coverage through Changshu Road, Jing'an Temple, and Xikang Road, and connect to South Shanghai Station and broader Puxi.
  • Didi Widely available and reliable

    Didi pickups are fast in Jing'an at almost any hour. Useful for late nights, large grocery shops, and reaching destinations between metro lines.

Renting

Housing in Jing'an

Jing'an has a mix of modern high-rise apartments, older walk-up buildings, and some serviced apartment options. It is not the cheapest area, but the supply is high and quality is generally reliable.

  • Type Mostly modern apartments and some older buildings

    Large modern apartment complexes with lift, security, and facilities are common near Jing'an Temple. Streets further north like Jiaozhou Road have older walk-up buildings with more character at lower prices.
  • Price Mid-to-high range for Shanghai

    A modern 1-bedroom in central Jing'an typically costs more than comparable apartments in Changning or Minhang, but you pay for location and convenience. Prices vary significantly by street and building age.
  • Lease 12-month leases are standard

    Most landlords prefer 12-month leases. Some serviced apartments offer shorter terms at a premium. Agents typically charge one month's rent as a fee. Residence registration is required within 24 hours of moving in.

Honest assessment

Trade-offs to consider

  • Pro Best transport links and international amenities

    Nowhere in Shanghai is easier to set up in quickly. Everything you need is within walking distance or a short metro ride.
  • Pro Easy to understand for newcomers

    The grid street layout, clear metro signage, and density of English-friendly services make early weeks significantly easier.
  • Con Higher cost than most other areas

    You pay a premium for centrality. Families needing larger apartments often get better value in Changning, Minhang, or Jinqiao.
  • Con Less character than the FFC or older Xuhui

    Jing'an is modern and convenient, but lacks the tree-lined lanes and heritage architecture that makes the Former French Concession feel distinctive.
