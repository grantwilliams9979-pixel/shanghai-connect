Living in Jinqiao, Shanghai.

International schools, compound living, green space, and more room than anywhere in Puxi. Jinqiao is where most expat families in Pudong plant their first roots.

Overview International schools Transport Housing

Best for

Families who prioritise school access, space, and a quieter pace

Jinqiao is a sub-district of Pudong, roughly 20 kilometres east of central Shanghai. It has developed over decades into one of Shanghai's largest international residential areas — driven primarily by the proximity of several well-regarded international schools and the availability of villa compounds with facilities that central Shanghai apartments rarely offer. It is a very different lifestyle from Jing'an or the FFC: more suburban, more car-dependent, but significantly more space for the same budget.

At a glance

What Jinqiao is like

Location

Pudong, northeast of city centre

Jinqiao sits in northern Pudong, between the inner ring road and the outer ring road. It is closer to Pudong Airport than to People's Square — plan transport accordingly.

  • Roughly 30 to 50 minutes to central Puxi by metro or car
  • Closer to Pudong Airport (PVG) than most of Shanghai
  • Adjacent to Zhangjiang Hi-Tech Park — useful for tech sector workers
  • Good access to Lianyang and other nearby Pudong expat areas
Vibe

Suburban and compound-focused

Jinqiao is defined by gated compounds — low-rise villas, townhouses, and apartment blocks with shared facilities including pools, gyms, tennis courts, and playgrounds. The street-level experience is quieter and more car-oriented than central Shanghai.

  • Large gated compounds with security and facilities
  • More green space and quieter streets than Puxi
  • International supermarkets and restaurants nearby
  • Less walkable for casual city browsing
Best for

Who chooses Jinqiao

Jinqiao is almost exclusively chosen for family and school reasons. Single professionals and couples without children rarely choose it over central Shanghai options.

  • Families with children at nearby international schools
  • Expats whose offices are in Pudong or Zhangjiang
  • People who prefer compound living with facilities
  • Those who want more space and a lower-density environment

The main reason families choose Jinqiao

International schools nearby

Jinqiao and the surrounding Pudong area have a high concentration of international schools serving the expat community. School access is the primary driver for most families who choose this area.

  • Check Confirm school place availability before choosing accommodation

    International school places in Pudong can be limited and competitive. Confirm enrolment before signing a lease — your child's school should determine your target neighbourhood, not the reverse.
  • Transport Many schools provide bus routes across Shanghai

    Most international schools run school bus networks that cover wider parts of the city. Confirm whether your compound is on a bus route before assuming your child will need local school access.
  • Timing Apply early — waiting lists are common

    Popular international schools in Pudong frequently have waiting lists, especially at primary level. Start the application process as soon as your relocation is confirmed, ideally 6 to 12 months before arrival.

Getting around

Transport from Jinqiao

Jinqiao's transport situation is the most common trade-off families accept. Metro access is limited compared to Puxi, and most daily logistics rely on Didi or a personal vehicle.

  • Line 6 The main metro option — Jinqiao Road station

    Line 6 connects Jinqiao Road station toward Century Avenue and interchange hubs in central Pudong. From Jinqiao Road, reaching Puxi requires a transfer, typically adding 20 to 30 minutes.
  • Didi The most practical daily option

    Most Jinqiao residents use Didi for school runs, grocery shops, and reaching the metro interchange or office parks. Journey times vary significantly with traffic, especially during morning and evening rush hours.
  • Airport Closer to Pudong Airport than most of Shanghai

    Pudong Airport (PVG) is roughly 20 to 30 minutes by car from most Jinqiao compounds — significantly faster than from central Puxi. Useful for frequent travellers.
  • Car Consider whether you need a car

    Many Jinqiao expat families keep a car or use a driver for school runs and weekend activities. Compound parking is typically included or available. Decide early whether your budget and lifestyle need a vehicle.

Renting

Housing in Jinqiao

Jinqiao offers the best value for floor space in the broader Shanghai expat market. Families can often afford a 3 or 4 bedroom villa or townhouse for the cost of a smaller apartment in central Jing'an.

  • Type Villas, townhouses, and compound apartments

    Gated compounds offering detached villas, row houses, and apartment blocks with shared facilities are the dominant housing type. Facilities typically include a pool, gym, playground, and 24-hour security.
  • Space Significantly more space for the same price as central Shanghai

    A 3-bedroom villa or townhouse in Jinqiao typically offers much more floor space than a comparable budget would buy in Jing'an or the FFC. This is the area's primary value proposition for families.
  • Lease Compound management handles most maintenance

    Unlike self-managed older apartments in Puxi, most Jinqiao compounds have on-site management for repairs, cleaning common areas, and handling utilities. This simplifies day-to-day property issues.
